Spicy Bulgogi Beef

The Marinade:

  • 2 tsp Minced garlic
  • 2 tsp Fish sauce
  • 2 tsp Sesame oil
  • 2 tsp Gochugaru (red pepper powder)
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp Gochujang (red pepper paste)

Whole ingredients:

  • 1/2 lb beef (ground or sliced)
  • 1/2 medium onion sliced
  • Green onion chopped (for garnish)
  • Sesame seeds (for topping)

Marinade the beef and onions together and set aside until ready to cook. Then cook on medium to high heat until the meat is cooked and sugars in the sauce caramelizes and thickens.

Garnish with chopped green onion and sesame seeds before serving.

Consuming and Creating

Cooking is an art because it combines creativity, technique, and sensory experience to transform raw ingredients into a dish that delights the palate and engages the senses. Just as an artist uses colors and textures to evoke emotions, a chef blends flavors, aromas, and presentations to create a culinary masterpiece that reflects personal expression and cultural heritage. The process requires intuition, experimentation, and a deep understanding of ingredients, much like the meticulous practice found in traditional forms of art, leading to dishes that can tell stories, celebrate occasions, or simply provide comfort.

The Breadth of Origin

Food's cultural character is shaped by a combination of historical influences, local ingredients, cooking techniques, traditions, and communal practices. It reflects the identity of a community, telling stories of migration, trade, and adaptation to the environment. Religious beliefs, social norms, and rituals also play significant roles, infusing dishes with symbolic meanings and making them integral to celebrations and daily life. Ultimately, food serves as a medium for cultural expression and connection among people.
